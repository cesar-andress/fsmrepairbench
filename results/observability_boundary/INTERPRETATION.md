# Observability boundary interpretation

## Progressive oracle surfaces

- **S0** (state): detection 49.5%, saturation 50.5%, participation 19.4%, inflation 50.4 pp.
- **S1** (state, action): detection 61.3%, saturation 38.7%, participation 30.3%, inflation 38.6 pp.
- **S2** (state, action, guard): detection 61.3%, saturation 38.7%, participation 30.3%, inflation 38.6 pp.
- **S3** (state, action, guard, event): detection 61.3%, saturation 38.7%, participation 30.3%, inflation 38.6 pp.

## Boundary readout

- Spectral participation rises from 19.4% to 30.3% across S0→S3, indicating observability surface affects localization denominators.
- Detectable-only random repair floor remains ≤0.2% on all surfaces.

## Answer (manuscript-ready)

Under progressively richer oracle surfaces (S0→S3), saturation inflation contracts as more faults become detectable and fewer remain oracle-saturated, while the detectable-only random repair floor stays near zero. The observability confound ceases to be practically important for repair ranking once saturation inflation falls below roughly 5–10 pp and cohort-wide CRR approaches the detectable-only rate; on the frozen 1k cohort this transition occurs between S0 and S3 at the field-visibility ladder tested here.
